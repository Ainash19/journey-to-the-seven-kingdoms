The Seven Kingdoms

The Vale

Perched above the clouds, sealed behind impassable peaks — the kingdom that only sky and stone could hold.

House Arryn's Eyrie

The Vale of Arryn sits behind the Mountains of the Moon, reachable only through treacherous passes and sky cells that drop away into nothing. It is the most defensible kingdom in Westeros — and its remote, cloud-piercing character was captured on screen through Northern Ireland's dramatic coastline, the jagged heights of Iceland, and the medieval cityscapes of the Montenegrin Adriatic.

For the traveller, the Vale's filming locations offer some of the most rewarding scenery of any Game of Thrones destination - dramatic without being inaccessible, storied without feeling overrun.

Filming Location

Six destinations shaped by heights, stone, and sky.

Meteora
Thessaly, Greece

The Moon Door.

While the set was built in Belfast, the high-altitude aerial shots of the Eyrie were inspired by or filmed at the rock pinnacles of Meteora. Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Meteora is renowned for both its natural beauty and cultural significance. The monasteries, built between the 14th and 16th centuries, were constructed atop immense sandstone pillars to provide safety, solitude, and spiritual refuge for monks.

Photo credit: Wikipedia
Cave Dale
Castleton, Derbyshire, England (Peak District)

Knights of the Vale.

Hidden behind the village of Castleton in England's Peak District, Cave Dale is a dramatic dry limestone valley with steep, almost vertical cliffs rising over 50 metres. Formed by glacial meltwater and later shaped by collapsing underground caverns, the dale has a rugged, gorge-like entrance that opens into a quieter, rolling landscape beyond.

Photo credit: Wikipedia
Ypapanti Monastery
Thessaly, Greece

Sky Cells.

Clinging to the side of a vertical rock face, Ypapanti Monastery feels suspended between earth and sky. Its remote and dramatic setting mirrors the quiet, elevated world of the Vale, where height is both protection and peril.

Photo credit: Wikipedia
Meteora
Thessaly, Greece

The Moon Door.

The sheer cliffs, narrow access paths, and breathtaking elevation create a landscape that feels suspended between earth and sky. This unique combination of natural grandeur and human ingenuity makes Meteora one of the most extraordinary and otherworldly locations in Europe.

Photo credit: Wikipedia
Great Meteoron Monastery
Thessaly, Greece

The Eyrie.

Rising high above the valley on a massive stone pillar, the Great Meteoron Monastery captures the isolation and power of the Vale. Surrounded by sheer drops and endless sky, it reflects the unreachable, fortress-like nature of House Arryn’s mountain stronghold.

Photo credit: Wikipedia
Meteora
Thessaly, Greece

High as Honour.

Balanced on narrow rock formations, Meteora creates a landscape of height, exposure, and danger. With steep cliffs and open edges, it closely reflects the sky cells of the Vale—where prisoners stood on the edge of nothing, with only air between them and the fall below.
