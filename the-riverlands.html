The Seven Kingdoms

The Riverlands

A land of crossings and conflicts — where rivers meet, armies march, and no House holds power for long.

Where Rivers Rule

The Riverlands are the crossroads of Westeros - flat, fertile, and perpetually contested. Ruled by House Tully from Riverrun, this is the kingdom that bore the brunt of every war. On screen, its waterlogged landscape was captured in the lush Croatian countryside, the emerald hills of Northern Ireland, and Iceland's river-laced valleys.

For travellers, the Riverlands' filming locations span three remarkably different countries - united by their greenness, their historical depth, and their sense of being places where great things once happened.

Filming Location

Where the rivers bend and history runs deep.

Gosford Castle
Markethill, County Armagh, Northern Ireland

"Wait for me, Cat."

Built in the 19th century in a striking Norman revival style, Gosford Castle is one of the largest historic buildings in Northern Ireland. Surrounded by the woodlands of Gosford Forest Park, its towers, battlements, and grand stone façade give it the imposing presence of a true medieval fortress, despite its relatively modern origins.
Over time, the castle has served many roles—from aristocratic residence to military barracks—before being restored and converted into private residences. Its dramatic architecture and secluded forest setting made it a perfect filming location for Game of Thrones, where it appeared as Riverrun, the ancestral seat of House Tully.

Krka National Park
Šibenik-Knin County, Dalmatia, Croatia

Riverrun.

Flowing through the heart of Dalmatia, Krka National Park is a vast natural reserve known for its cascading waterfalls, emerald pools, and limestone canyons. The Krka River winds through the landscape, forming a series of spectacular falls—most famously Skradinski Buk, a wide natural pool fed by multiple cascades.
Although often compared to Croatia’s UNESCO-listed parks, Krka itself is not a UNESCO World Heritage Site. However, its rich blend of natural beauty and history—including monasteries, old mills, and walking trails—makes it one of the country’s most iconic and accessible природных landscapes.

Una River Valley
Western Bosnia-Herzegovina

Riverrun.

The rural landscapes around the Una River provided some of the pastoral Riverlands scenes — abandoned farmsteads, hay meadows, and river crossings that gave the war-torn middle kingdoms their lived-in, weathered character. The region is little known to international visitors and deeply rewarding to explore.

Þjórsá River Plains
South Iceland

Riverrun.

Iceland's longest river bisects a wide, moss-edged plain that doubled in several seasons as a transitional Riverlands landscape — flat, open, and quietly ominous. The surrounding lava fields and glacier-fed floodplain create a strangely melancholy beauty that the show used to suggest a land between battles.

Krka River
Dalmatia, Croatia

Riverrun.

Flowing through limestone canyons in southern Croatia, Krka River is known for its clear emerald waters and a series of breathtaking waterfalls. Over thousands of years, the river has carved its way through the landscape, creating natural pools, cascades, and lush green surroundings rich in wildlife.
The river forms the heart of Krka National Park, where wooden walkways and historic watermills allow visitors to explore its unique ecosystem up close. Its combination of flowing water, stone, and greenery makes it one of Croatia’s most striking natural landscapes.
