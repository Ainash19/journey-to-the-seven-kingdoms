About This Guide

The Real lands behind the Legend

Game of Thrones conjured an entire world from the page and brought it to screen across some of the most breathtaking landscapes on Earth. From the frozen shores of Iceland to the sun-scorched walls of Dubrovnik, the Seven Kingdoms were never truly fictional — they were always waiting to be found.

This guide is for the traveller who wants more than a highlight reel. Here you will find the real-world locations behind each kingdom, the scenes they carried, and what it feels like to stand where winter came and dragons flew.

All locations are real, publicly accessible places. This is a fan travel guide created for educational and inspirational purposes, inspired by HBO's Game of Thrones.

Why Visit the Real Seven Kingdoms?

Because some places becoma more than locations - they become legend.

Living History

Every filming location is already steeped in centuries of real history - Game of Thrones simply added another layer to places that were already remarkable.

Unexpected Destinations

Iceland's lava fields, Morocco's medinas, and Northern Ireland's Antrim Coast - the show quietly mapped out some of Earth's most underappreciated landscapes.

Cinematic Encounters

Standing on the cliffs of Dubrovnik or at the Dark Hedges in Antrim - familiar places feel suddenly cinematic, even on a quiet Tuesday afternoon.
