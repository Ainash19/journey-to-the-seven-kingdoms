Dorne

Unbowed, Unbent, Unbroken

Dorne is Westeros's warmest, most fiercely independent kingdom - a land that was never truly conquered, where women rule alongside men and the water gardens are the most beautiful place in the world. On screen, Dorne was brought to life almost entirely in Spain: the Moorish palace complex of Seville's Real Alcazar, the sun-bleached plains of Andalucia, and the Roman ruins of Italica.

Travelling the real Dorne is travelling through layers of Spanish history - Roman, Moorish, medieval - all concentrated in one of Europe's most beautiful and culturally rich regions. Seville alone is worth the journey.

Real Alcázar de Sevilla
Seville, Andalucía, Spain

Water Gardens of Dorne.

Still the official residence of the Spanish Royal Family when visiting Seville, this UNESCO World Heritage palace is a masterpiece of Moorish-Gothic-Renaissance architecture. Its coloured tilework, horseshoe arches, and fragrant gardens are the closest thing on Earth to how George R.R. Martin imagined Sunspear.

Baños de Doña María de Padilla
Seville, Andalucía, Spain

Water Gardens.

Golden light reflects across still water beneath a series of perfectly repeating Moorish arches, creating a hypnotic symmetry that feels both serene and mysterious. Located deep within the Real Alcázar of Seville, these underground baths date back to the 14th century and are named after María de Padilla, the mistress of King Pedro I.
The quiet, reflective space-hidden below the palace-captures a sense of secrecy, elegance, and power, where water, light, and architecture merge into one of the most atmospheric interiors in Spain.

Alcázar Gardens
Seville, Andalucía, Spain

"We don't hurt little girls in Dorne."

Bathed in sunlight and framed by elegant Moorish arches, the gardens of the Real Alcázar unfold in a series of perfectly manicured paths, fountains, and palm-lined courtyards. The combination of geometric hedges, warm stone, and lush greenery creates a tranquil yet regal atmosphere that reflects centuries of Andalusian design.
Winding walkways lead through shaded corridors and open terraces, where water and light play across the landscape. The harmony between architecture and nature gives the gardens a sense of calm, refinement, and hidden luxury—making them one of the most enchanting spaces in southern Spain.

Alcázar Gardens
Seville, Andalucía, Spain

“You want revenge?”

Dorne in Game of Thrones was primarily filmed at the Alcázar of Seville in Spain, which served as the Sunspear palace and its Water Gardens in season 5. Specific areas used include the Pool of Mercury, the Baths of María de Padilla, and the Hall of Ambassadors. Additional Dorne scenes were filmed in the Alcazaba of Almería

Alcazaba of Almería
Almería, Andalucía, Spain

“Vengeance. Justice. Fire and Blood.”

Rising above the city of Almería, the Alcazaba of Almería is one of the largest and most impressive Muslim fortresses in Spain. Built in the 10th century by the Caliph Abd ar-Rahman III, the vast complex includes multiple walled enclosures, towers, gardens, and the remains of a palace, all designed to defend the city and control the surrounding coastline.
The fortress unfolds across terraces and courtyards, where open spaces contrast with powerful defensive walls overlooking the sea and desert landscape. Its scale, history, and strategic position create a sense of strength and isolation, making it one of the most striking historical landmarks in southern Spain.

Photo credit: Wikipedia
Castle of Zafra
Guadalajara, Castilla-La Mancha, Spain

"Promise me, Ned!"

Perched dramatically atop a narrow rocky ridge in the remote landscapes of Castilla-La Mancha, Castle of Zafra is one of Spain's most striking and isolated fortresses. Built in the 12th century, its stone walls rise directly from the rock, blending seamlessly with the surrounding cliffs and creating the impression of a castle carved from the mountain itself.
Exposed to wind and sky on all sides, the fortress feels both wild and untouchable, far removed from any settlement. Its rugged setting and natural defenses give it a powerful, almost mythical presence, making it one of the most visually dramatic medieval sites in Spain.
